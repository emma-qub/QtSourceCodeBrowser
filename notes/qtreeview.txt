<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;"><span style=" font-size:14pt; font-weight:600;">Using a QFileSystemModel</span></p>
<p style="-qt-paragraph-type:empty; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;"><br /></p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">When using a QFileSystem model, expanding the QTreeView nodes can be pretty confusing.</p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">The most likely reason for an expand not to work is because the <span style=" font-weight:600;">loading is lazy</span>. Meaning that every</p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;"><span style=" font-weight:600;">sub directories and files are not loaded at the beginning</span>. That makes sense, since this kind of</p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">model can contain a HUGE number of items. Think of a tree view that lists your whole computer directories</p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">and files when the root directory is / (Linux) or C:\ (Windows).</p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">Thus, the <span style=" font-weight:600;">expand method is threaded</span> and any home made method that tries to <span style=" font-weight:600;">expand recursively</span></p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">with only a for or a while loop <span style=" font-weight:600;">is meant to fail</span>. Either one has to connect the directoryLoaded signal</p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">to a slot that goes on expanding from this point:</p>
<p style="-qt-paragraph-type:empty; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;"><br /></p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;"><span style=" font-family:'mono'; color:#ffffff; background-color:#404244;">  _                                                                             <br />  connect(fileSystemModel, SIGNAL(directoryLoaded(</span><span style=" font-family:'mono'; font-weight:600; color:#5caa15; background-color:#404244;">QString</span><span style=" font-family:'mono'; color:#ffffff; background-color:#404244;">)),                    <br />          this, SLOT(expandDirectory(</span><span style=" font-family:'mono'; font-weight:600; color:#5caa15; background-color:#404244;">QString</span><span style=" font-family:'mono'; color:#ffffff; background-color:#404244;">)));                                <br />                                                                                </span></p>
<p style="-qt-paragraph-type:empty; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px; font-family:'mono'; color:#ffffff;"><br /></p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">or use the QTreeView setCurrentIndex() method combined with the convenient index() method from the</p>
<p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">QFileSystemModel that can retrieve the index from an absolute path:</p>
<p style="-qt-paragraph-type:empty; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;"><br /></p>
